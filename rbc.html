HACKATHON - Spring 2021

RBC Footprint

A learning tool designed for the RBC hackathon challenge, “How can RBC Rewards Programs better help consumers make environmental decisions?”.

Design Overview

Footprint is an extension of the RBC Rewards app that provides learning opportunities for RBC Reward members to educate themselves about the environment, and to learn about sustainable practices they can adopt in their daily lives while earning points that can be redeemed towards a purchase of their choice.

Framing the Problem

Young adults play an important role in raising awareness about environmental issues. They believe they have the ability to make a difference, but lack the support and motivation to do so.

Instead of trying to create something that young adults can do without a second thought, I asked my team how might we make information on sustainability more accessible for young credit card owners in order to better equip them to make environmentally conscious decisions?

Prescedent Studies

Looking at learning methods and companies with loyalty programs and looking at educational material, I conducted a precedent study to understand how loyalty programs are used to engage consumers, and to gain a better understanding of the various ways people learn and retain information. From my findings, I generated a list of ideas that focused on guidance, learning, and positive reinforcement and presented it to my teammates.

Ideation, wireframe, and communication

As a team, we grouped and combined ideas together to form our solution. After, I quickly sketched out potential layouts of the solution and generated a set of 20+ wireframes. As the hacking phase was only 36 hours long, my team and I worked simultaneously together to ensure the idea was submitted by the deadline. A challenge occurred during the wireframing stage, where the developers were unsure of the flow of interactions presented in my sketches. Understanding that these developers have a different background than I do, I used a combination of wireframes and flowcharts to communicate how and where the user would click to proceed to the next screen.

From our research, we found that RBC has at least 3 different apps in the app store. This led us to create an extension of the app in order to reduce the amount of apps that users have to download. As it would be an extension of the app, I referenced the current RBC Rewards App while sketching, wireframing, and creating mockups to ensure this added feature is consistent with the rest of the app.

Reflection

The idea was presented to 4+ RBC judges and awarded 1st place out of 15 teams. I am used to working with a group of designers, so this experience allowed me to work in a cross-disciplinary team. Working with a cross-disciplinary team taught me how to better empathize with my teammates who are not designers, to understand how our process and roles are interconnected, and it allowed me to form a deeper sense of appreciation and communication with my team. I am extremely proud of my team and how fluidly we worked together and took ownership of our roles in this short time.

Going forward, if I had more time to work on this, I would perform user tests with current RBC Reward members to see if this is something they would use and how to improve it. I would also add different types of learning methods, such as a mini-game where one would have to sort out recycling, compost, and garbage in the correct bin, and perform an A/B test to see which types of learning methods are preferred.
